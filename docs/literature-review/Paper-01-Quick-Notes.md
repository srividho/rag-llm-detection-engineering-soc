# Paper 01 – Summary

## Title

AI-Augmented SOC: A Survey of LLMs and Agents for Cyber Defense

---

## Publication

**Authors:** Ahmed Abdel Hady et al.

**Year:** 2025

**Publisher:** MDPI

**Paper:**
https://www.mdpi.com/2624-800X/5/4/95

---

# Research Theme

AI-Augmented Security Operations Centers (SOC)

---

# Why did I read this paper?

To understand the current research landscape of AI, LLMs, RAG, and AI agents in Security Operations Centers and identify future research opportunities.

---

# Problem Statement

Modern Security Operations Centers face several operational challenges:

- Alert fatigue caused by large volumes of security alerts.
- Shortage of experienced SOC analysts.
- Increasing complexity of cyber attacks.
- Manual investigation processes requiring multiple knowledge sources.

---

# Proposed Solution

This paper surveys existing research on AI-assisted SOC operations and categorizes how AI technologies are currently being applied across different SOC activities.

Instead of proposing a new framework, it provides a comprehensive overview of the current state of research.

---

# Main Technologies

- Artificial Intelligence
- Large Language Models (LLMs)
- Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)
- AI Agents
- MITRE ATT&CK
- Threat Intelligence
- SIEM

---

# Key Contributions

- Comprehensive survey of AI in SOC operations.
- Categorizes AI applications across different SOC tasks.
- Highlights emerging research trends.
- Identifies future research opportunities.

---

# Limitations

- Survey paper only.
- No implementation.
- No experimental evaluation.
- Does not propose an integrated SOC framework.

---

# Research Gap

Most existing research focuses on individual SOC activities such as:

- Alert triage
- Log analysis
- Threat intelligence
- Incident response

Very few studies integrate these capabilities into a single workflow that supports both investigation and detection engineering.

---

# Relation to My Research

This paper validates the motivation behind my research.

It confirms that Detection Engineering remains an important research area and that integrating RAG, LLMs, contextual knowledge, and detection engineering into one framework is still an open research direction.

---

# Key Takeaways

- AI should assist analysts rather than replace them.
- SOC workflows remain fragmented.
- Context-aware AI is becoming increasingly important.
- Detection Engineering presents an opportunity for future research.
- My proposed framework fits well within the future research directions identified by the authors.

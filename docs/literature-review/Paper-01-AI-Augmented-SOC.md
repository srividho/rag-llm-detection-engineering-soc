# Research Theme

AI-Augmented Security Operations Centers (SOC)

---

# Background

Security Operations Centers (SOCs) are responsible for continuously monitoring organizational infrastructure, identifying security threats, investigating incidents, and coordinating response activities. However, modern SOCs face increasing operational challenges due to the rapid growth of cyber threats, alert volumes, and the shortage of experienced cybersecurity analysts.

Recent advances in Artificial Intelligence, particularly Large Language Models (LLMs) and AI agents, have created new opportunities to support SOC analysts by automating repetitive tasks and improving investigation efficiency. This survey reviews the current research landscape in this area and discusses how AI technologies are being integrated into SOC operations.

---

# Research Problem

The paper identifies several major challenges currently affecting Security Operations Centers.

The first challenge is the overwhelming number of alerts generated by security monitoring systems. Analysts spend a considerable amount of time distinguishing genuine threats from false positives, resulting in alert fatigue and delayed investigations.

Another challenge is the increasing complexity of cyber attacks. Modern incidents often require analysts to correlate information from multiple sources such as SIEM logs, MITRE ATT&CK, threat intelligence feeds, vulnerability databases, and previous incident reports.

In addition, organizations continue to experience a shortage of skilled cybersecurity professionals, making it difficult to scale SOC operations efficiently.

These challenges motivate the need for AI-assisted SOC workflows.

---

# Proposed Solution

Instead of proposing a single implementation, this paper presents a comprehensive survey of how Artificial Intelligence, Large Language Models, Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), and AI agents are currently being applied within Security Operations Centers.

The survey categorizes existing research into several operational areas, including:

- Alert triage
- Log analysis
- Threat intelligence
- Incident investigation
- Report generation
- Detection engineering
- Autonomous SOC operations

The authors discuss how AI technologies can reduce manual effort while supporting analysts throughout the investigation lifecycle.

---

# Technologies Discussed

The survey covers several emerging technologies used in AI-assisted cybersecurity.

- Artificial Intelligence (AI)
- Large Language Models (LLMs)
- Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)
- AI Agents
- Security Information and Event Management (SIEM)
- MITRE ATT&CK Framework
- Threat Intelligence Platforms

Rather than focusing on implementation details, the paper explains how these technologies contribute to different SOC activities.

---

# Key Contributions

One of the major contributions of this survey is providing a structured overview of AI applications across Security Operations Centers.

The paper develops a taxonomy that categorizes existing research according to different SOC functions, making it easier to understand where AI is currently being adopted and where further research is needed.

Another important contribution is the discussion of future research directions, including autonomous SOCs, multi-agent systems, explainable AI, and context-aware security analysis.

---

# Strengths

This paper provides one of the most comprehensive overviews of AI-assisted SOC research currently available.

It successfully connects multiple research areas that are often studied independently, including LLMs, RAG, AI agents, threat intelligence, and detection engineering.

Because the paper reviews a broad range of existing work, it serves as an excellent starting point for understanding the current research landscape.

---

# Limitations

As a survey paper, the study does not propose or evaluate a new AI framework.

There is no experimental implementation or performance evaluation, making it difficult to compare different approaches quantitatively.

Although the paper discusses detection engineering, it does not propose an integrated framework that combines investigation, contextual reasoning, and detection improvement within a single workflow.

---

# Research Gap

The survey highlights that most existing AI-assisted SOC solutions focus on individual operational tasks such as alert triage, log analysis, or incident investigation.

There is relatively little research exploring how these capabilities can be integrated into a unified framework that supports analysts throughout both investigation and detection engineering activities.

This represents a significant opportunity for future research.
